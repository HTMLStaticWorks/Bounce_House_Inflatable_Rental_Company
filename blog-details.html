5 Safety Tips for Backyard Parties

Alex Johnson
May 10, 2026 • 5 min read

Planning a backyard party with a bounce house is one of the best ways to keep children entertained. However, safety should always be your top priority.

Here are five essential safety tips every parent and event organizer should know before the inflatables arrive:

1. Constant Adult Supervision

Never leave the children unattended. A responsible adult should always be watching the entrance and exit of the bounce house to prevent horseplay and overcrowding.

2. Separate by Size

Try to group children of similar age and size together. Larger kids can accidentally bump into smaller ones, leading to potential injuries. If you have a wide range of ages, consider setting a schedule for different groups.

3. Remove Jewelry and Sharp Objects

Ensure all children remove shoes, glasses, jewelry, and any sharp objects from their pockets. Not only does this protect the children, but it also prevents punctures to the inflatable.

4. Watch the Weather

Inflatables should not be used in high winds (typically over 15-20 mph) or during thunderstorms. If the weather turns, immediately have everyone exit and deflate the unit.

5. Set Ground Rules

Before the fun begins, explain basic rules: no flipping, no wrestling, and no climbing on the walls or mesh windows. Clear rules help prevent accidents before they happen.
